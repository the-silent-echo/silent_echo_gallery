This work as a quiet choreography suspended between gravity and dream. A figure appears at its center, poised yet weightless, her body unfolding like a gesture remembered rather than performed. She does not stand on the ground; instead, she seems to emerge from a fan-like structure that opens behind her—something between a shell, a dress, and a threshold. It holds her, but also releases her, as if she is both being born and dissolving at the same time. Around her, the space is carefully arranged, almost ceremonially. Two vertical walls of flowering plants rise on either side, enclosing her within a garden that feels both intimate and theatrical. Above, a pale, ambiguous moon hangs low, while a single candle descends from the sky, its flame replaced by stillness. These elements do not obey logic, but they feel precise, as if each has been placed to mark a moment rather than describe a place.
